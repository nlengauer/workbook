Fashion magazines and their websites place image and text in really interesting ways. I discovered Schema magazine when researching for another subject and immediately noticed the way in which they displayed their pages. The transitions between the two are seamless and the interfaces are often engaging and fun as well as effective in conveying information. It provided a valuable resource when thinking of ways to depict a workbook in the form of a physical book. The whole magazine is scanned and placed in the centre of the screen, and once clicked on, the spreads open and become full-sized.



I discovered Internet Brain and loved the way the interactivity was helpful for gathering information. I really wanted to add a hover feature in my project and liked the way in which this website used the feature to start an auto scroll.



I stumbled upon this website portfolio of portugese designer Marina Chimeno and loved her use of colour, interactivity and especially, her cursor. I couldn't screenshot it, as each time the cursor would disappear, but it was a glyph of a few hypens in a face-ish shape. I knew I wanted a special cursor for my project, at least for the landing page and this website let me see what was possible. I liked the way she set up her page, with all the different ways in which she presented her work and progress, not settling on the same design for each category of work.



This website's header is an animation on loop spelling out the artist's name. I really enjoyed how this movement felt smooth and fluid, and also kept the eye engaged on an otherwise static website. This was where I decided to add an animation feature to my landing page, yet the easiest thing for me to manage was to change the bacakground colour through smooth transitions. This gives the page a playful edge, something I wanted to explore in my designs.
